Feeding Your Cat

Cats are obligate carnivores and require a diet high in protein. Feed them high-quality cat food and ensure fresh water is always available.

  • Feed kittens 3-4 times daily
  • Adult cats typically do well with 2 meals per day
  • Avoid feeding cats dog food or human food

Health & Veterinary Care

Regular vet check-ups are essential for your cat's health. Key health considerations include:

  • Annual vaccinations
  • Regular flea and worm treatments
  • Spaying/neutering if not breeding
  • Dental care to prevent periodontal disease

Enrichment & Play

Cats need mental and physical stimulation to stay happy:

  • Provide scratching posts to satisfy natural instincts
  • Rotate toys to keep them interesting
  • Create vertical spaces for climbing
  • Interactive play sessions daily

Litter Box Tips

  • Have one more litter box than the number of cats (e.g., 2 boxes for 1 cat)
  • Place boxes in quiet, accessible locations
  • Scoop daily and change litter weekly
  • Use unscented litter - cats prefer it
  • If your cat stops using the box, consult a vet as it may indicate health issues
